# The 100-Day Markeet Growth & Virality Playbook

**Goal:**  
Reach **1,000+ verified UI students** before the commencement of the 2025/2026 academic session.

**Primary Channel:** WhatsApp  
**Secondary Effects:** Offline conversations, screenshots, forwards, campus buzz

---

## Core Principle

> People don’t join because something exists.  
> They join because **people around them are joining**.

Markeet’s growth depends on **engineered social proof, consistency, and community ownership**, not just awareness.

---

## Psychological Foundations Behind the Strategy

This plan deliberately leverages five proven behavioral principles:

### 1. Social Proof

People follow numbers, not explanations.

- Daily user count
- Public progress tracking
- “X students have joined” framing

### 2. Commitment & Consistency

Once people engage emotionally, they keep supporting.

- “Day X of 100” framing
- Ongoing progress narrative

### 3. Community Identity

Strong UI identity is a major motivator.

- “Built by UI students, for UI students”
- Collective responsibility messaging

### 4. Loss Aversion

People fear missing out more than they desire benefits.

- “Join early or regret later”
- “This could have worked if we supported it”

### 5. Authority Through Persistence

Daily visibility signals seriousness and legitimacy.

- Consistent posting builds trust
- Persistence = credibility

---

## The 100-Day Structure

The campaign is divided into **four strategic phases**.

---

## Phase 1: Awareness & Legitimacy (Days 1–14)

**Objective:**  
Make Markeet widely known and taken seriously.

**What people should be thinking:**

> “I keep seeing this Markeet thing.”

### Actions

- One WhatsApp broadcast per day
- Post in rotating class, hostel, and faculty groups
- Maintain a calm, informative tone

### Messaging Focus

- What Markeet is
- Why it exists
- Student-built narrative
- Problems with current buying/selling methods

Consistency here establishes authority.

---

## Phase 2: Social Proof & Pressure (Days 15–45)

**Objective:**  
Convert awareness into active participation.

**What people should be saying:**

> “People are actually joining.”

### Key Changes

- Daily user count becomes prominent
- Light pressure introduced
- Encouragement to share the platform

### Psychological Tool

**Public scoreboard effect**  
People want to be counted.

Example framing:

> “UI has thousands of students. We’re counting who shows up.”

---

## Phase 3: Belonging & Movement (Days 46–75)

**Objective:**  
Transform Markeet from an app into a student movement.

**What people should be saying:**

> “Markeet is our own.”

### New Elements

- “Early supporters” language
- Community ownership emphasis
- Recognition of consistent supporters

This phase leverages boredom and free time during break to create belonging.

---

## Phase 4: Urgency & Countdown (Days 76–100)

**Objective:**  
Drive last-mile signups before resumption.

**What people should be saying:**

> “Resumption is close.”

### Tactics

- Countdown language
- “Last X days” framing
- Future regret messaging

Loss aversion is strongest at this stage.

---

## Core Daily Message Template (WhatsApp)

This structure remains consistent throughout the 100 days:

Markeet — the online marketplace for UI students.

Day {X} of 100
Goal: 1,000 UI students before resumption
Current users: {number}

{1–2 short psychologically driven paragraphs}

We still have {100 − X} days to make this work together.

Join. Share. Tell one UI student.
👉 [link]

Repetition builds legitimacy.

---

## Distribution Rules (Critical)

### Do Not:

- Spam the same group repeatedly
- Post everywhere at once
- Engage in public arguments

### Do:

- Rotate groups daily
- Encourage forwards
- Allow others to repost organically

Virality increases when the message stops sounding like it comes from one person.

---

## Growth Multiplier Strategy

By approximately Day 30:

- Identify **5–10 consistent supporters**
- Ask them privately to repost daily updates

This shifts Markeet from a single voice to a distributed movement.

---

## Measurement & Iteration

Track daily:

- New signups
- Message formats that perform best
- Groups that convert most effectively

This is an ongoing experiment, not guesswork.

---

## Final Note

The current period — no exams, no classes, students at home — is a **rare growth window**.

If Markeet does not grow during this phase, it will not be because the idea or platform is weak, but because momentum was not deliberately engineered.

Planning first is the right move.
