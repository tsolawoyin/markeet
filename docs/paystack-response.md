## 1. KYC Information Collected from Third-Party Sellers

All sellers on Markeet must be verified University of Ibadan students. During registration, we collect the following:

- Full legal name
- University email address (must be a valid `@stu.ui.edu.ng` or `@dlc.ui.edu.ng` domain)
- Phone number (validated against Nigerian mobile formats)
- Hall of residence
- Course of study
- Institution

Account activation requires email verification — users must click a confirmation link sent to their university email before they can access the platform.

## 2. Enhanced Due Diligence on Vendors

Our primary due diligence mechanism is **university email verification**. Since only students with active University of Ibadan email accounts can register, this serves as institutional identity verification — the university has already performed its own KYC during student admission. Beyond this:

- All seller profiles display verifiable identity signals (hall of residence, course, institution) visible to buyers.
- We maintain an admin moderation system with the ability to ban users and remove listings.
- A seller reputation system tracks ratings, reviews, completed orders, and completion rates, with badge tiers based on performance.

## 3. Acceptable Use Policy

We are currently finalising our formal Acceptable Use Policy document. In the interim, our platform enforces acceptable use through:

- Restricting access to verified university students only
- Admin moderation tools for banning violating users and removing prohibited listings
- A reporting mechanism for flagging inappropriate listings
- Community guidelines agreed to during registration

We will share the completed policy document once finalised.

## 4. Fulfillment Model

Markeet is **not** a fulfilment centre. We are a peer-to-peer marketplace where sellers deliver directly to buyers. Since all users are students on the same university campus, transactions typically involve in-person meetups at convenient locations (halls of residence, departments, etc.). There is no warehousing, shipping, or logistics infrastructure managed by the platform.

## 5. Payment Handling

Yes, we intend to receive payments on behalf of vendors through an escrow system. The flow works as follows:

1. Buyer places an order and makes payment through the platform
2. Funds are held in escrow (not released to the seller)
3. Seller delivers the item to the buyer
4. Buyer confirms receipt using a secure PIN
5. Payment is then released to the seller's wallet
6. Seller can withdraw funds to their bank account

This model protects both parties — buyers are guaranteed delivery before funds are released, and sellers are guaranteed payment upon confirmed delivery.

## 6. Dispute Management

Disputes are managed through the following process:

- Either party can flag an order as disputed, which freezes the held funds
- Our admin team reviews the dispute, examining order details, communication, and delivery evidence
- Based on the review, the admin either releases payment to the seller or processes a refund to the buyer
- Users who repeatedly violate platform rules are banned from the platform

We also offer a full refund guarantee if a transaction fails or cannot be resolved in the buyer's favour.
